## Overview

With almost no prep time, I produced a roughly 5:30 senior-year film for American Youth Academy's 2026 graduation — the piece that played for parents, teachers, and attendees during the ceremony.

The goal was to show what the graduating class actually learned and lived: their skills, their knowledge, their Islam, and their deen. Rather than a montage, I interviewed the seniors themselves and shaped their answers into a story of how Aya Academy helped the class of 2026 grow into confident, grounded young Muslims ready for the next chapter.

I recorded the interviews on-site with controlled lighting and a treated vocal chain, then pieced the story together from what the graduates said. Because of the event's urgency, I leaned on the school's previous footage and b-roll instead of capturing my own — letting the students' voices carry the narrative.

In post I handled the full pipeline myself in DaVinci Resolve: story edit, dialogue cleanup with compression, EQ, and noise reduction, plus color grading to tie the archival and interview material together into one cohesive film.
